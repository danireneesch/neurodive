Certified Autist

2026-02-09

one time i told someone i was autistic and she said, don’t say that about yourself! oh. ok. to be fair, that was before i became an official, card-carrying autist certified, verified, bona fide i can show you my credentials see this paper right here? F84.0 Autism Spectrum Disorder it took me 27 years to earn that i am very proud! i believe the doctor’s exact words were there’s definitely something going on there after i did too well on the adhd test to get a prescription for uppers but still managed to check off every trait in the DSM V when i was in grade school the other kids would say i was an alien and it really did feel like it sometimes …oftentimes it turns out i’m a real earthling after all i just didn’t get the built-in user manual that most other humans apparently come with i have to gingerly feel my way through existence hoping i do the right things and don’t do the wrong things because people aren’t always empathetic when something like how to know it’s time to leave is right there in chapter 7, section C, line 2 but i’m still sitting there as they awkwardly smile and common phrases that actually mean something else entirely are in a bulleted list on pages 194-197 i have to scribble everything down as i figure it out and hope i don’t annoy anyone too much in the process most of my life i felt defective but mostly these days i find it pretty neat that i get to perceive the world in a slightly different way and i make a point of observing and finding out all the ways other people experience the world because there’s really no default perspective and reality is a bit fuzzy on the edges when you look close enough and maybe it’s all a bit of everything all at once and all of our infinite configurations work together to show off all the infinite facets of the universe and i think that’s pretty cool
